  • IBM is of course massive (~400,000 employees) and the Bluemix team alone is pretty big (estimating it to be in the thousands)
  • We're not in the process of validating, we are in the process of executing at scale. As a result, our team has to work in lockstep, with many teams shipping different aspects of the product that integrate seamlessly, provide a consistent user experience, and are extraordinarily stable and secure.

The Workflow

This is a N:M relationship. The design team alone is over 100 people, and we're interfacing with hundreds more engineers, product managers, etc.

A Single Source of (Design) Truth

Bluemix has approached this challenge by asserting a single source of truth for design, essentially shipping Bluemix designs as a single product that engineering teams "subscribe" to. So we can ship new versions of the interface just like iOS ships new versions, provide release notes, offer backwards compatibility etc. We can ship without telling our engineering teams and their work updates automatically (well its supposed to)

Our Product

  • Prototyping and design assets (Sketch Files) distributed to design teams with the mandate that new features and products be created and assembled from those assets. If something is missing, teams are asked to design a solution and present it to the design systems team so it can be integrated into the design system. Then, one team's solution can be shared immediately with all other teams.
  • A set of styles and self invoking Javascript classes exposed through a well defined API, along with defined HTML structures for components. Engineers subscribe to this API, which is valid only when the proper HTML structure is used, to render their work visually. They only need to take care of wiring things together, not building interactions.
  • When the time comes, the design team releases a new version of the design system, documents breaking changes, and requests that engineering teams take the time to upgrade the dependency to the new version. They'll be prevented from deploying their work until they have upgraded the dependency.
